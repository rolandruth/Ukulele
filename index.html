What is chord transposition?

Transposition means shifting a song's chords up or down by a number of semitones — the smallest interval in Western music. If a song is in the key of G and you need to play it in A to suit a singer's voice, you transpose up 2 semitones.

This tool handles all 12 notes of the chromatic scale including enharmonic equivalents (F# = Gb, A# = Bb) so the output always uses the most musically natural spelling for the chosen key.

Frequently asked questions

Does it work for ukulele?

Yes — chord transposition is instrument-agnostic. The tool shifts chord names; how you finger those chords on a uke, guitar, or piano is up to you.

Is my chord sheet saved?

Yes, locally. Your input and all settings are saved to your browser's localStorage. They persist after refresh but never leave your device.

How many semitones to go from G to A?

Two semitones up. The key indicator at the top shows your original key and current transposition at a glance.

Does it work offline?

After your first visit the page is cached by your browser and works without an internet connection — ideal for gigs and rehearsal rooms with no Wi-Fi.
